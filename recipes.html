Day-Before Prep Plan

What to Do the Day Before

Goal: reduce day-of stress

These steps can all be done the day before Thanksgiving so that the actual cooking day (starting around 10:30 AM and finishing the meal at 3:00 PM) is much smoother.

Turkey: Thawing & Optional Brining

  • Make sure turkey is fully thawed in the fridge. For a 12–13 lb bird, allow about 3 days of fridge time.
  • If you like a brined turkey:
    • Mix a brine: about 1 cup kosher salt + 1/2–1 cup sugar per gallon of water, plus herbs, garlic, peppercorns, citrus slices.
    • Submerge turkey in a large food-safe container or brining bag, keep refrigerated.
    • Brine for up to 12–24 hours, then remove turkey, pat dry, and store uncovered in the fridge to dry the skin overnight (helps crispiness).

Pies (Pumpkin & Pecan Chocolate)

  • Fully bake both pies the day before.
    • Follow the pumpkin pie and pecan chocolate pie recipes below.
    • Let them cool completely, then cover loosely and refrigerate overnight.
    • On Thanksgiving, bring to room temp before serving, or warm slices briefly if desired.
  • This frees up oven space and mental bandwidth on the main day.

Cranberry Sauce

  • Cook the fresh cranberry sauce totally the day before.
  • Cool, transfer to a container, and refrigerate. It actually tastes better after resting overnight.

Chopping & Prep Work

  • Wash and chop red onions for stuffing and other dishes; store in airtight containers.
  • Wash and trim green beans (you can even blanch them and cool, then store covered in the fridge).
  • Peel and cube sweet potatoes, store submerged in cold water in the fridge (to prevent browning), then drain well before roasting.
  • Measure out sugar and spices for pies into small containers or labeled bags.

Tabletop & Misc

  • Lay out serving dishes, utensils, and labels (e.g., “stuffing”, “green beans”, “gravy”).
  • Chill drinks that need to be cold.
  • If kids are helping, assign simple tasks (stirring, carrying napkins, etc.).

Doing these steps the day before means that on Thanksgiving you mostly focus on: roasting the turkey, finishing sides, reheating, and enjoying the meal.

Main Thanksgiving Dinner

Roast Turkey with Herbs Uses: turkey, herbs, onions, butter, citrus

Prep: ~30 min Cook: ~3 hr Rest: ~30 min

From your list: Ferndale frozen turkey, organic mixed herbs, red onions, lemons, navel orange, butter (on hand), broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole turkey (about 12–13 lb), thawed
  • 4–6 tbsp butter, softened
  • 1–2 red onions, quartered
  • 1 lemon, halved
  • 1 navel orange, halved
  • Handful of mixed herbs (thyme, rosemary, sage, etc.)
  • 1–2 cups chicken broth (Field Day broth)
  • Salt and black pepper
  • Optional: garlic, paprika, olive oil

Steps

  1. Thaw the turkey: If frozen, thaw in the fridge (about 24 hours per 4–5 lb). Make sure it’s fully thawed before cooking.
  2. Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C).
  3. Prepare the turkey:
    • Remove neck and giblets from cavity (save for stock if you like).
    • Pat turkey dry with paper towels inside and out.
  4. Season cavity:
    • Sprinkle salt and pepper inside.
    • Stuff with lemon halves, orange halves, onion quarters, and a handful of herbs.
  5. Butter and herbs on the outside:
    • Loosen skin over breast gently with your fingers.
    • Rub softened butter under the skin over the breast meat.
    • Rub remaining butter over the outside of the turkey.
    • Season generously with salt, pepper, and chopped herbs.
  6. Set up roasting pan:
    • Place turkey breast-side up on a rack in a roasting pan.
    • Pour 1–2 cups broth into the bottom of the pan.
  7. Roast:
    • Roast at 325°F, basting with pan juices every 45–60 minutes.
    • Approx time: about 3 hours for your bird.
  8. Check doneness:
    • Insert a meat thermometer into the thickest part of the thigh (not touching bone).
    • Done at 165°F (74°C). If skin darkens too fast, tent loosely with foil.
  9. Rest:
    • Remove from oven and let rest at least 20–30 minutes before carving.

Creamy Mashed Potatoes Uses: russet potatoes, milk, butter

Prep: ~15 min Cook: ~30 min

From your list: russet potatoes, Rosenberger whole milk, butter.

Ingredients

  • About 3 lb russet potatoes (from your 4.28 lb total)
  • 4 tbsp butter (or more to taste)
  • 1/2–1 cup milk, warmed
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Peel potatoes (optional for more rustic) and cut into chunks.
  2. Place in a pot, cover with cold salted water, and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce to a gentle boil and cook 15–20 minutes until very tender.
  4. Drain well and return potatoes to the warm pot.
  5. Add butter and a splash of warm milk. Mash until smooth.
  6. Add more milk as needed to reach desired creaminess.
  7. Season generously with salt and pepper. Keep warm until serving.

Roasted Sweet Potatoes Uses: organic sweet potatoes/yams

Prep: ~10 min Cook: ~30–40 min

From your list: organic sweet potatoes/yams.

Ingredients

  • 1.5–2 lb sweet potatoes
  • 2–3 tbsp oil or melted butter
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: cinnamon, smoked paprika, maple syrup

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (204°C).
  2. Peel sweet potatoes and cut into 1-inch cubes.
  3. Toss with oil, salt, pepper, and any spices you like.
  4. Spread on a parchment-lined baking sheet in a single layer.
  5. Roast 25–35 minutes, tossing halfway, until browned and tender.
  6. Optional: drizzle with a little maple syrup before serving.

Green Bean Casserole Uses: green beans, creamy soup, crispy onions

Prep: ~20 min Bake: ~35 min

From your list: local green beans, Pacific cream of mushroom soup, Food Club crispy onions, milk, optional corn/peas.

Ingredients

  • 1–1.5 lb fresh green beans, trimmed and cut in half
  • 1 can Pacific organic cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/3–1/2 cup milk
  • 1 cup crispy onions (plus extra for topping)
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).
  2. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and blanch green beans 4–5 minutes. Drain and rinse briefly with cold water.
  3. In a bowl, mix mushroom soup, milk, and a pinch of salt and pepper.
  4. Stir in blanched green beans and about half of the crispy onions.
  5. Pour mixture into a baking dish.
  6. Bake 20–25 minutes until bubbling.
  7. Top with remaining crispy onions and bake 5–10 more minutes until golden.

Fresh Cranberry Sauce Uses: cranberries, sugar, orange

Prep: ~5 min Cook: ~15 min

From your list: fresh cranberries, navel orange, sugar (pantry).

Ingredients

  • 1 bag (12 oz) fresh cranberries
  • 1 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 3/4 cup water or apple juice
  • Zest and juice of 1 orange (from your navel orange)

Steps

  1. Rinse cranberries and pick out any soft or shriveled ones.
  2. In a saucepan, combine water/juice, sugar, orange zest, and orange juice.
  3. Bring to a simmer, stirring to dissolve sugar.
  4. Add cranberries and cook over medium heat 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until most berries have popped and mixture thickens.
  5. Taste and adjust sweetness (add more sugar if needed).
  6. Cool to room temperature; sauce will thicken more as it cools. Chill until serving.

You also bought canned cranberry sauce – serve that alongside this fresh version for variety.

Simple Herb Stuffing Uses: stuffing mix, onions, herbs, broth

Prep: ~20 min Bake: ~40 min

From your list: Arnold stuffing mix, red onions, mixed herbs, Field Day broth, butter.

Ingredients

  • 2 boxes herb stuffing mix
  • 1–2 red onions, diced
  • 2–3 tbsp butter or oil
  • 2–3 cups chicken broth (warm)
  • Chopped mixed herbs
  • Salt and pepper (if needed – stuffing mix is often already salty)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).
  2. In a pan, sauté diced onions in butter/oil until softened and lightly golden.
  3. In a large bowl, add stuffing mix, sautéed onions, and herbs.
  4. Pour in warm broth gradually, tossing with a spoon, until cubes are moistened but not soggy.
  5. Transfer to a greased baking dish and cover with foil.
  6. Bake 25–30 minutes, then uncover and bake an additional 10–15 minutes for a crisp top.

Gluten-Free Cornbread Uses: Bob’s GF cornbread mix

Prep: ~10 min Bake: ~20–25 min

From your list: Bob’s Red Mill gluten-free cornbread mix, eggs, milk, butter.

Steps (follow the box + this simplification)

  1. Preheat oven to the temperature indicated on the cornbread mix (usually around 375–400°F).
  2. In a bowl, combine the cornbread mix, eggs, milk, and melted butter according to the box instructions.
  3. Stir just until combined (some small lumps are OK).
  4. Pour batter into a greased baking pan.
  5. Bake until golden and a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean (usually 20–25 minutes).
  6. Cool slightly, slice, and serve warm.

Glazed Baby Carrots Uses: rainbow baby carrots

Prep: ~5 min Cook: ~10–15 min

From your list: organic rainbow baby carrots.

Ingredients

  • 1 bag baby carrots
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar or honey
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Place carrots in a pan, cover with water, and simmer 6–8 minutes until just tender. Drain.
  2. Return pan to medium heat, add butter and brown sugar/honey.
  3. Toss carrots in the glaze until coated and slightly glossy.
  4. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Serve warm.

Warm Apple “Cider” Drink Uses: apple juice

Prep: ~5 min Heat: ~15–20 min

From your list: Field Day organic apple juice.

Ingredients

  • Apple juice
  • Optional: cinnamon sticks, cloves, orange slices

Steps

  1. Pour apple juice into a pot.
  2. Add a cinnamon stick, a few cloves, and optional orange slices.
  3. Warm gently over low heat until steaming (do not boil).
  4. Remove whole spices before serving. Serve warm in mugs.

Pumpkin Pie

Pumpkin Pie (using Farmers organic pumpkin) Uses: pumpkin can, pie crust, eggs, cream/milk, sugar & spices

Prep: ~20 min Bake: ~50–60 min Cool: ~2 hr

From your list: Farmers organic pumpkin pie can, Midel GF pie crust, eggs, Rosenberger milk or heavy cream, sugar and spices from pantry, whipped cream.

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust (Midel GF graham or similar)
  • 1 can (about 15 oz) Farmers organic pumpkin
  • 2–3 large eggs
  • 1 cup milk or a mix of milk + cream
  • 1/2–3/4 cup sugar (mix of white and brown if you like)
  • 1–1.5 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg
  • Pinch of salt
  • Whipped cream for serving

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C).
  2. Place pie crust in a pie dish if it’s not already in one. Crimp edges if needed.
  3. In a large bowl, whisk pumpkin, sugar, spices, and salt until smooth.
  4. Whisk in eggs one at a time.
  5. Add milk/cream and whisk until fully combined (mixture will be pourable).
  6. Pour filling into the pie crust.
  7. Bake at 425°F for 15 minutes.
  8. Reduce oven to 350°F (177°C), and continue baking 35–45 minutes, until the center is mostly set (a slight jiggle in the middle is okay).
  9. Cool completely at room temperature, then chill if you prefer firmer slices.
  10. Serve with whipped cream.

Pecan Chocolate Pie

Pecan Chocolate Pie Uses: pecan halves, pie shell, chocolate chips, eggs, sugar/syrup

Prep: ~20 min Bake: ~50–60 min Cool: ~2 hr

From your list: pecan halves, Wholly 9" pie shell, Field Day semi-sweet chocolate chips, eggs, sugar, (corn syrup or extra sugar), butter (from pantry).

Ingredients

  • 1 pie shell (Wholly whole 9" pie shell)
  • 1–1.5 cups pecan halves
  • 1/2–1 cup chocolate chips
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup light corn syrup or 3/4 cup sugar + 1/4 cup water
  • 4 tbsp melted butter
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (optional but nice)
  • Pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F (177°C).
  2. Place the pie shell on a baking sheet.
  3. Spread chocolate chips evenly over the bottom of the crust.
  4. Arrange pecan halves on top (you can reserve some to place decoratively on top later).
  5. In a bowl, whisk eggs until smooth.
  6. Add corn syrup (or sugar + water mix), melted butter, vanilla (if using), and a pinch of salt. Whisk until fully combined.
  7. Pour the mixture gently over the pecans and chocolate in the crust.
  8. If desired, place a few pecan halves neatly on top for decoration.
  9. Bake 45–55 minutes, until the filling is set around the edges and the center has just a slight jiggle.
  10. If the crust browns too quickly, cover the edges with foil.
  11. Cool completely before slicing so the filling can firm up.
